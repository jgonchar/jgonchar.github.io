Annette Lake

Snoqualmie Region | Snoqualmie Pass

7.5 miles roundtrip
Elevation gain: 1800 ft. | Highest Point: 3600 ft.
Difficulty: Strenuous
Brewery within 5 miles

Lake Serene and Bridal Veil Falls compete for attention on this trail. Either would be a worthy destination by themselves and combined they are a great day on the trail. From the parking lot, where a Northwest Forest Pass is required, the trail follows an old road bordered by towering salmonberry bushes and decidous trees. The berries ripen in early to mid June, and the best one are found high above your head. Read more >>

Little Si

Snoqualmie Region | North Bend

4.7 miles roundtrip
Elevation gain: 1300 ft. | Highest Point: 1500.0 ft.
Difficulty: Moderate
Brewery within 1 mile

Companion piece to neighbor and big brother Mount Si, the small rocky bluff known as Little Si is a moderate grade 4.7 mile trail located in the hike rich North Bend area. Although relatively steep inclines bookend this hike, this primarily North/South route is perfect for beginners who are looking to get back in shape, while still accommodating veterans with opportunities to cut away on the Boulder Garden Loop (at .3 miles and .5 miles) to merge with the more challenging Mount Si trail. Mount Si

Snoqualmie Region | North Bend

8.0 miles roundtrip
Elevation gain: 3150 ft. | Highest Point: 3900.0 ft.
Difficulty: Strenuous
Brewery within 1 mile

There are many reasons – besides its proximity to Seattle – why Mount Si is the most popular hike in the state: beautiful views, wildlife (some of it not so wild), old growth forest, and a dramatic summit worthy of a more remote peak. Rising dramatically above the Snoqualmie Valley, it serves as a literal and figurative gateway to the Cascades for thousands of hikers. Read more >>

Snow Lake

Snoqualmie Region | Snoqualmie Pass

7.2 miles roundtrip
Elevation gain: 1800 ft. | Highest Point: 4400 ft.
Difficulty: Moderate
Brewery within 2 miles

If you believe that good things only come to those who toil and weat (or if you don't like to share the outdoors with other hikes), steer clear of Snow Lake. Its translucent depths, tucked underneath the uppermost 2,000 feet of Chair Peak, are removed from the highway crossing Snoqualmie Pass by just a short trail with minimal elevation gain. Elitists need not apply - Snow Lake is the most visited lake in the Alpine Lakes wilderness and righly so! Read more >>
